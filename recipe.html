Recipe for Cameroonian Jollof Rice

Prep Time: 15 Minutes

Total Time: 1 Hour

Serving Size: 4 People

Print

Instructions

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly in cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute or until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste and let it cook for a few minutes until it darkens in color.
  1. Add the blended tomato puree, paprika, stock cubes, cayenne pepper, thyme, and curry powder. Mix well and allow it to simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally until the sauce thickens and the oil starts to separate.
  2. Add the washed rice and stir it into the tomato sauce, ensuring the rice is well coated.
  3. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, add the bay leaves, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Bring it to a boil.
  1. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the rice simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  2. While the rice is cooking, steam the mixed vegetables until they are tender but still crisp.
  1. Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork, and gently stir in the steamed vegetables.
  2. Cover the pot and let it sit for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Serve your delicious Cameroonian Jollof rice as a main course or as a side dish with your choice of protein, such as grilled chicken or fish.
